Why is the book called Showdown Symphony?

A. The Showdown: One autumn night, while searching for seeds to feed her family, a clever mother mouse encounters a cat and an owl on the prowl for a meal of their own. Who will get to eat?

The Symphony: The story is told in five symphonic movements (allegro, adagio, andante, scherzo, & allegretto), with each movement developing plot and character through a unique poetic stanza pattern. The accompanying symphony also develops the story through its tempo, motif, and instrumentation.

How long is the book?
  • Approximately 40 pages of full-color illustrations
  • Designed to be read in one or two sittings
  • Durable hardcover built for repeat reading
All other rhyming picture books I’ve read have just one poetic form throughout the whole book. Why does Showdown Symphony change poetic form so many times?
A. Each movement in Showdown Symphony uses a poetic meter and rhyme scheme that matches the characters and action described in that movement. For example, the Allegro section describes the mouse scurrying around to find food, and the rhyme scheme is trochaic tetrameter - it sounds like a ticking clock. By contrast, the cat’s Adagio movement has short lines that are spondaic: each syllable is stressed, which makes the lines much slower - like the cat.
